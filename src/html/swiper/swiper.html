Day 1

Day 1: Introduction to First Aid and Basic Life Support

Morning Session: Welcome and Course Introduction:

  • Introduction to the training program
  • Overview of the curriculum
  • Importance of First Aid in emergencies

Afternoon Session: Legal and Ethical Considerations:

  • Understanding Good Samaritan laws
  • Consent, confidentiality, and ethical considerations in First Aid
  • Responsibilities of a first responder

Day 2

Day 2: Assessing and Addressing Critical Situations

Morning Session: Scene Assessment and Primary Survey:

  • Techniques for assessing emergency situations
  • Conducting a primary survey
  • Effective communication during emergencies

Afternoon Session: CPR and AED Training:

  • Hands-on CPR techniques for adults, children, and infants
  • Proper usage of Automated External Defibrillators (AEDs)
  • Real-life simulation exercises

Day 3

Day 3: Managing Traumatic Injuries

Morning Session: Wound Care and Bleeding Control:

  • Managing various types of wounds
  • Techniques for controlling bleeding
  • Dressing, bandaging, and wound care essentials

Afternoon Session: Fractures, Sprains, and Dislocations:

  • Identifying fractures, sprains, and dislocations
  • First aid for musculoskeletal injuries
  • Immobilization techniques

Day 4

Day 4: Handling Medical Emergencies

Morning Session: Recognizing and Responding to Medical Emergencies

  • Signs and symptoms of heart attacks, strokes, seizures, and other medical emergencies
  • First aid interventions for common medical crises
  • Dealing with respiratory distress and anaphylaxis

Afternoon Session: Burns, Electrical Injuries, and Environmental Emergencies:

  • First aid for burns, electrical shocks, and chemical exposures
  • Managing heatstroke, hypothermia, and other environmental emergencies
  • Outdoor first aid considerations

Day 5

Day 5: Special Populations and Mental Health First Aid

Morning Session: First Aid for Children, Infants, and the Elderly:

  • Pediatric first aid and CPR techniques
  • Special considerations for elderly individuals
  • Addressing unique challenges in these populations

Afternoon Session: Mental Health First Aid:

  • Recognizing signs of mental health issues
  • Providing initial support and reassurance
  • Crisis de-escalation techniques

Day 6

Day 6: Practical Assessments and Certification

Morning Session: Practical Skills Demonstrations:

  • Participants demonstrate CPR, choking relief, wound dressing, and other skills learned
  • Participants complete a written test to evaluate their theoretical knowledge
  • Practical assessments to evaluate competency in various first aid skills

Evening Session: Certificate Award Ceremony:

  • Distribution of certificates to participants who successfully pass the assessments
  • Closing remarks and acknowledgment of participants' achievements
